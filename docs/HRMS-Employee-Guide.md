# HRMS - Employee Handbook & App Guide

*A friendly, complete walkthrough of your company HR portal - what every screen does and how to use it. Written for a brand-new employee. Read it once and you'll know the whole system.*

---

## 1. Welcome - what this app is

This is your company's **HRMS (Human Resource Management System)**. It's your single place to:

- Mark your attendance (with a selfie), apply for leave, and see your shifts.
- View and download your **payslips**, request loans/advances, and file your tax declaration.
- Claim expenses and travel, track your goals, take courses, and complete tasks.
- Read announcements, answer surveys, and see who won **Rewards & Recognition** each month.
- Keep your profile, documents, and requests in order - and, when the day comes, submit your resignation.

There are two "portals":

- **My Portal (employee)** - everything about *you*. This guide is about this portal.
- **Admin Portal (HR/Admin)** - for HR, managers, and leadership. If you're a **Manager**, you'll also get a couple of team features inside your employee portal (approving your team's leave, seeing your team's presence).

You can use the HRMS on the **web** (browser) and on the **Android mobile app** - most day-to-day things work on both.

💡 **The 5-minute tour:** Sign in → your **Dashboard** greets you → punch in from **Attendance** → check your **Leave** balance → glance at your latest **Payslip** → read **Announcements** and this month's **Rewards & Recognition** winners. That's the daily loop.

## 2. Signing in & your roles

- Log in with the **email and password** HR gave you. (New joiners often get a default password - change it as soon as you can.)
- Forgot your password? Use the **"Request a password reset"** link on the login screen; HR will reset it for you.
- Your **role** decides what you see. Most people are an **Employee**. Some are also a **Manager** (you approve your team's leave and see their attendance). Senior leaders (CEO/MD) and HR use the admin portal.

⚠️ You **cannot** change your own login email or password from inside the app (except Super Admins). To change them, you raise a **Change Request** (see §12) - HR approves it.

## 3. Your Dashboard (home screen)

The Dashboard is your landing page. From top to bottom you'll typically see:

- **Welcome banner** - a greeting with two quick stats (**Pending Requests**, **Leave Days Left**) and shortcut buttons **Add Request** and **Apply Leave**.
- **Announcements** - company notices; you can close each one (it stays closed for you).
- **Rewards & Recognition** - the monthly winners banner (see §9). Shows photos; you can close it.
- **Surveys** waiting for your response, and **Interviews** you're scheduled to conduct.
- **Wishes for you** - birthday/anniversary wishes colleagues sent you.
- **My Attendance heatmap** - a calendar-style grid of your attendance.
- **Snapshot cards** - remaining leave, pending requests, your latest **net pay**, and your employee code/designation/department.
- If you're a **Manager**, a **team status** strip shows your reports' presence.

💡 Everything on the dashboard is a shortcut - tap a card to jump into the full module.

## 4. Attendance & Time

### 4.1 Attendance - punching in and out
**What it's for:** recording that you were at work each day.

**What you do:**
- Tap **Check In** in the morning and **Check Out** when you leave. You get **one check-in and one check-out per day**.
- Each punch requires a **selfie** (a quick photo from your camera) - this is mandatory.
- Each punch also captures your **GPS location**. The app waits a few seconds for an accurate fix and shows your coordinates with a map link. If the fix is poor or you're far from the office, it warns you - but it **still lets you punch** (it just records the distance).
- Tick **Work From Home (WFH)** if you're working remotely - WFH punches are never flagged as "outside office."
- At checkout you can tick **Mark as Half Day** if it was a half day.
- After check-in a **live timer** counts your working time; it freezes when you check out.

**Where you see your history:** a month/year filter plus a table of every day - status (Present, Absent, Half Day, Weekly Off, Holiday, **On Leave**), your in/out times with location links, hours worked, and remarks.

⚠️ **"Late" means checking in after 10:00 AM.** This matters for your pay - see §5.

### 4.2 The "Lateness & leave" card (very important)
Right on your Attendance screen there's a card that shows, for the current month:

- **Late arrivals** - e.g. `2 / 5` (you've been late 2 times out of the 5 free ones), with "N over the limit" if you've crossed 5.
- **Expected late deduction** - the rupee amount you'd lose this month for extra late days, with the maths (e.g. `3 × ₹200/day`).
- **Paid leave used** - e.g. `1 / 2` of your monthly paid-leave quota, and "N day(s) LOP" if you've gone over.
- **Leave incentive** - extra pay you'll earn for **unused** paid leave this month.

💡 If HR hasn't set up your salary yet, the card shows the counts but notes that the *amounts* will finalise once your salary is configured.

### 4.3 My Shifts
A **read-only** list of your rostered shifts - date, shift name, and timing (shown in 12-hour AM/PM). Nothing to do here except check when you're scheduled.

### 4.4 Regularization (fixing attendance mistakes)
**What it's for:** correcting a missed or wrong punch.

- Tap **+ New Request**, pick a date and a **type** (Missing Punch, Wrong Time, Forgot Check-in, Forgot Check-out, On Duty, Other), enter the correct times, and a reason.
- Track it in the history table: **Pending → Approved / Rejected**, with the reviewer's note.
- When HR approves, your attendance for that day is corrected automatically.

## 5. ⭐ Pay rules you MUST know (leave & lateness)

This company has two simple pay rules that directly affect your salary. Learn them well - they're applied automatically every month.

### Rule 1 - 2 paid leaves a month (and unused leave becomes extra pay!)
- Every month you get **2 paid leave days**.
- **If you take 2 or fewer** approved leaves → those days are **paid** as normal.
- **If you take more than 2** → each extra day is **Loss of Pay (LOP)** - that day's salary is deducted.
- **If you take fewer than 2** → the **unused** days are **converted into extra pay** for that month, called **"Leave Incentive."** It's one day's salary per unused day.
- This is **settled every month** - unused leave does **not** carry forward to the next month.

**Worked examples:**
- 🟢 You take **0 leaves** this month → you earn **+2 days of extra pay** (Leave Incentive).
- 🟢 You take **1 leave** → **+1 day** extra pay.
- 🟡 You take **2 leaves** → normal pay, no bonus, no deduction.
- 🔴 You take **3 leaves** → 2 are paid, **1 day is LOP** (unpaid).

### Rule 2 - 5 free late arrivals a month, then a penalty
- "Late" = you **checked in after 10:00 AM**.
- The **first 5 late days each month are free** (no penalty).
- From the **6th late day onward**, each extra late day costs:
  - **₹200 per day** if your **monthly Basic salary is below ₹25,000**, or
  - **₹400 per day** if your **monthly Basic is ₹25,000 or more**.

**Worked examples:**
- 🟢 You were late **4 times** → no penalty.
- 🔴 You were late **8 times**, Basic ₹20,000 → 3 extra late days × ₹200 = **₹600 deducted**.
- 🔴 You were late **6 times**, Basic ₹30,000 → 1 extra late day × ₹400 = **₹400 deducted**.

### Where you see all this
- Live, during the month → the **"Lateness & leave" card** on your Attendance screen (§4.2).
- Final, in your salary → your **payslip** will show a **"Leave Incentive"** line under earnings and/or a **"Late Arrival Penalty"** line under deductions.

⚠️ The card's numbers are **estimates** during the month. The **final** amounts are locked when HR runs and approves payroll.

## 6. Leave & Holidays

### 6.1 Leave
**What it's for:** requesting time off and tracking your balances.

- **Balance cards** show your buckets: **EL** (Earned Leave), **CL** (Casual), **SL** (Sick), **ML** (Maternity) - balance, used, and total each.
- **Apply for Leave:** choose a **type** (EL, CL, SL, ML, PL = Paternity, COMP = Comp-off, LOP), toggle **Half-day** (and pick First/Second Half), set **From/To** dates, and add a reason.
  - A half-day counts as **0.5 day**; a full request counts every day inclusive.
- **My Requests** shows each request's type, dates, days, and **status** (Pending / Approved / Rejected / Cancelled), plus a visual of the **approval chain** and any decision note.
- **Cancel** a request while it's **Pending** or even after it's **Approved**. Cancelling an approved leave gives your balance back.

**How approval works (the chain):** your request climbs your **reporting hierarchy** - your manager, then their manager, and so on up to the first **CEO/MD**, who gives final approval. HR is kept informed. If you have no manager, HR decides. Your leave **balance is only deducted at final approval** (and only for EL/CL/SL/ML).

💡 **Approved leave auto-marks your calendar.** Once your leave is fully approved, those days automatically show as **On Leave** in attendance (weekends and holidays are skipped, and any day you actually worked is left alone). This is also what feeds the "2 paid leaves" rule in §5. (An approved **LOP** leave shows as unpaid.)

### 6.2 Holidays
There's no separate holidays page for you - company holidays appear in the **Calendar** (§9) and are automatically respected by attendance and pay rules.

## 7. Payroll & Money

### 7.1 Payslips
- See all your **Approved/Paid** payslips: period, gross, deductions, net, and status.
- **View** opens a detailed breakdown: working/paid/LOP days, every earning (including **Leave Incentive**), every deduction (including **Late Penalty**), gross/total/net, and payment date/reference.
- **Download PDF** for any payslip - the PDF also includes your **year-to-date** totals for the financial year.

### 7.2 Loans & Advances
- Tap **+ Request**, pick a **type** (Salary Advance, Personal Loan, Emergency, Other), enter the **principal** amount and a reason.
- Track status: **Pending → Approved → Active → Closed** (or Rejected).
- HR sets the EMI, tenure, and balance. Once your loan is **Active**, the **EMI is automatically deducted** from your salary each month (shown as "Loan Recovery" on your payslip).

### 7.3 Tax Declaration (Form 12BB)
- Pick the **Financial Year** (April–March; defaults to the current one) and your **tax regime** (Old/New).
- Enter your investment/expense amounts by section: **80C, 80CCD(1B), 80D, 24B (home loan interest), 80E, 80G, HRA rent, LTA, Other** - a live **Total declared** updates as you type.
- Add **proof links** (label + URL).
- **Save Draft** any time, then **Submit**. Once **Submitted** (or **Verified**), the form becomes **read-only**; HR can Verify or Reject with a note.

### 7.4 Expenses
- **+ New Claim:** choose a **category** (Travel, Food, Accommodation, Supplies, Medical, Communication, Other), enter the **amount** and **expense date**, plus merchant, description, and an optional receipt link.
- Track status: **Pending → Approved → Reimbursed** (or Rejected), with the reviewer's note.

### 7.5 Travel
- **+ New Request:** purpose, origin, destination, from/to dates, **mode** (Flight/Train/Bus/Car/Other), estimated cost, advance requested, and notes.
- If you already paid, fill the **reimbursement** block (amount paid, paid-on date, what it covers) and **upload the receipt/bill** (image or PDF).
- Track both the **request status** (Pending/Approved/Completed/Rejected) and the **reimbursement status** separately.

## 8. Performance & Learning

### 8.1 Goals
- See goals assigned to you (title, description, period, status).
- Update your **progress** with a **0–100% slider**. Your manager's review note and star rating appear once given. *(Managers/HR assign goals; you update progress.)*

### 8.2 My Reviews
- **Reviews assigned to me:** open **"Fill in review"** - score each competency **1–5 stars**, add comments, an overall rating, strengths, and areas to improve, then **Submit**.
- **Feedback about me:** reviews others wrote about you are shown **anonymously** (you can't see who said what) - competency scores, overall, strengths, improvements.

### 8.3 Learning (courses)
- **My Courses:** your approved enrollments with status (Enrolled / In Progress / Completed), a **deadline** chip, and progress bar. Tap **Start / Continue / Review** to open the player.
- **Course Catalog:** browse courses (category, duration, module count, deadline) and tap **Request to enroll** - enrollment needs HR approval, so you may see "Awaiting approval."
- **Course Player:** a Udemy-style player with the video/content on one side and the **curriculum** on the other; it tracks your module completion. There's a **"Report an issue"** option (video quality, audio, playback, content error, other).

## 9. Work Management

### 9.1 Tasks
- Your assigned tasks with title/description, project, **priority** (Low/Medium/High/Urgent), and due date.
- You update the **status** (Todo / In Progress / Review / Done). *(Admins create tasks.)*

### 9.2 Assets
- A **read-only** list of company assets issued to you - name/serial, tag, category, and the date assigned.

### 9.3 Documents
- A **"Missing required documents"** banner shows which documents you still need to upload.
- **Upload** a document: pick a category, choose one or more files (**PDF/JPG/PNG/WEBP/HEIC/DOC/DOCX, up to 5 MB each**), add an optional note.
- Some categories (like Offer Letter, Appraisal Letter) are **HR-only** - HR uploads those, not you.
- Track each document's status (**Submitted → Verified / Rejected**) with the reviewer's note, **Download** it, or **Delete** the ones you uploaded.

## 10. Engagement & Communication

### 10.1 Announcements
Company notices with a category badge (General, Policy, Event, Holiday, Benefits, Urgent), pinned markers, the author, and date. You can **dismiss** each from your dashboard banner.

### 10.2 Surveys & Polls
Active surveys/polls appear as cards (with a question count and an "anonymous" tag when applicable). Tap an unanswered one to respond. Answered surveys are marked **✓ Submitted**.

### 10.3 Calendar
A month grid of everything happening - **holidays, events, birthdays, work anniversaries, and interviews** - color-coded. Navigate by month and click a day for details.

### 10.4 ⭐ Rewards & Recognition (RNR)
Each month HR recognises top performers, and the winners appear as a **celebration banner** on your dashboard:

- **Employee of the Month** - one person for the whole company (photo, name, designation, department).
- **Key Achievers** - one person **per department** (photo, name, department).
- The banner shows for about **2 working days** after HR announces it, and you can **close** it any time.
- You'll also get a **notification** when the winners are announced.

💡 This is the company's way of celebrating great work - if you see your name, congratulations! 🎉

## 11. If you approve, lead a team, or interview

These appear for you when they're relevant:

- **Approvals** - if you're anyone's reporting manager, leave requests waiting for *your* decision land here. Approve or reject your rung; the request then moves up the chain. (If you never approve anyone, this is just empty.)
- **My Team** *(Managers)* - a read-only **presence board** of your direct reports (present / on leave / absent, with check-in/out times, WFH tags, hours). Approvals happen in the Approvals inbox.
- **My Interviews** - interview rounds where you're the interviewer. **Join** the meeting, **view the résumé**, set the round **result** (Pending/Scheduled/Cleared/Rejected), and save your **feedback**.
- **Onboarding** - your joining checklist with a progress bar. Move each task to **In Progress / Done**.
- **Org Chart** - a visual of the company's reporting structure.

## 12. Self-service & requests

### 12.1 Profile
- View your full profile: personal details, employment info, statutory IDs (PAN, UAN, etc. - sensitive ones are hidden), and bank details.
- **You can self-edit only two things without approval:** your **profile photo** and your **date of birth**.
- Everything else → use **"Request a change."**

### 12.2 Account & Requests (Change Requests)
- To change any other detail (or your email), raise a **Change Request**: pick the field, see the current value, enter the new value, add a reason.
- Track it: **pending → approved / declined**, with the current→requested values and HR's note. When approved, HR applies the change for you.

### 12.3 Complaints
- Raise a **confidential** complaint: choose the person it's **against**, add a subject and description.
- It's shared only with **CEO, HR, and Super Admin** - **never** the person it's about. Track its status (open / under review / resolved / dismissed).

---

## 13. Leaving the company (Resignation)

- Under **Resignation**, submit your resignation: **last working day**, **notice period** (default 30 days), and a **reason** (kept confidential).
- Once submitted, you'll see a read-only summary with your **HR contact** and the **status** (Pending → In Clearance → Completed, or Cancelled). Any changes after that are handled by HR.

## 15. Quick cheat-sheet & FAQ

- **How do I mark attendance?** Attendance → Check In (selfie + location) → Check Out.
- **When am I "late"?** Any check-in **after 10:00 AM**.
- **How many free lates do I get?** **5 per month**; after that ₹200/day (Basic < ₹25k) or ₹400/day (Basic ≥ ₹25k).
- **How much paid leave per month?** **2 days.** Unused converts to extra pay that month; more than 2 becomes LOP (unpaid). No carry-forward.
- **Where do I see my expected deductions?** The **"Lateness & leave" card** on the Attendance screen.
- **How do I apply for leave?** Leave → Apply → it goes up your manager chain for approval.
- **Where's my salary?** Payslips → View or Download PDF.
- **How do I change my email/password?** Raise a **Change Request** (email) - you can't self-edit those.
- **What can I edit myself?** Only your **profile photo** and **date of birth**.
- **How do I report a problem in a course?** Use **"Report an issue"** in the course player.
- **Who sees my complaint?** Only CEO/HR/Super Admin - never the person it's about.

Welcome aboard - you're all set! 🎉
